# Performance notes

## Musical Performance Fundamentals

### Know what your musical message/sound concept is
- Have a clear idea of the note pitches in your head
- Have a clear idea of what message you are trying to send is
- Lead with what you hear in your head
- Musical concept is like knowing how you are driving, and where you would like to drive

### Examining your Breathing Efficiency
- Have a quick fix when you feel you are not breathing properly
- Keep your pointer finger in front of your mouth and breath in loudly
- Lungs are made up of a plastic tissue and you fill them up with air like a balloon
- The moment you breathe in, the elasticity immediately wants to deflate, and will do so without any effort
- This will take your lungs from 100 percent full of air to about 66 percent. This is when the body gets involved, which is when tension is created, which is our enemy
- This is like the gas you are putting in the car, whether it is low octane or high octane racing fuel

### Eliminating Embouchure Tension
- Necessary to only use the exact amount of tension required to play the note. Anything else will affect your tone and range, and is just a bunch of unnecessary effort
- Try and master the overtone series so you know the best places to vibrate. Let it do its job, rather than trying to force it. It will tell you to respect the tube and each note will unfold
- This is similar to your engine, the way you vibrate your lips

### Utilize the Horn's Angle
- The angle of the airstream changes the degree of angle of the horn on your face
- In the middle register, it will come out like your bite
- In a low note, the angle will come up from the bite
- For the high register, the angle will be down from the bite
- Do what suits you best and let the airstream go where it naturally tends to go

### Shaping your Oral Cavity
- This is similar to singing. If you have a small mouth with your teeth clenched and your tongue at the top of it, you are not going to be able to get a good sound
- Have a resonant space within your mouth to produce the sound and let the horn amplify it
- Use low vowels and keep the tongue low in your mouth

### Fixing your Hand Position
- You want a hand position that will help you in all forms of horn playing, tuning, playing, holding, etc.
- Slightly bend your hand in and put it in.
